The problem

Your team's knowledge is trapped in meetings.

The average agency or consulting firm runs 15–25 client meetings per week. Each one produces commitments, decisions, context, and nuance that's critical to the relationship. Where does it all go? Into someone's memory. Into scattered notes. Into a Zoom recording nobody will ever watch.

Three months later, a client says "but you promised us X" — and your team spends 30 minutes searching Slack, email, and their own memory to figure out what was actually said. Sometimes they find it. Sometimes they don't. The client relationship pays the price either way.

Briefroom makes every meeting instantly searchable, permanently recalled, and automatically actionable. It's the institutional memory your team needs but nobody has time to build manually.

How it works

Record. Summarize. Index. Recall.

During

Automatic transcription

Briefroom joins your Zoom, Meet, or Teams calls (or ingests recordings). Speaker diarization identifies who said what. Transcription is real-time with 98%+ accuracy — including technical jargon, proper nouns, and your industry-specific vocabulary.

After

Smart summarization

Within minutes of the call ending, Briefroom generates a structured summary: key decisions, action items (with owners and deadlines), open questions, and client sentiment signals. The summary is written in your team's voice, not generic AI-speak.

Always

Semantic indexing

Every meeting is embedded into a vector database — meaning you can search by concept, not just keyword. "What did we discuss about the Q3 timeline?" works even if nobody said the word "timeline" in the meeting. Cross-references across meetings surface patterns and recurring themes.

Anytime

Instant recall

Ask Briefroom a natural language question in Slack: "What did we promise Acme about the Q3 launch?" It returns the answer with exact timestamps, speaker attribution, and links to the relevant part of the recording. Seconds, not 30 minutes of searching.
