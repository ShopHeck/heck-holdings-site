The problem

You're the last to know.

Your competitor dropped prices 12% last Tuesday. They launched a new product line on Thursday. They started running Facebook ads targeting your customers on Friday. You found out… Monday, when a customer mentioned it.

Manual competitive monitoring doesn't scale. You can't check five competitors' websites, ad libraries, review pages, and job postings every day. But Scout can. It runs daily sweeps, stores historical snapshots, detects material changes, and tells you what actually matters — not a 40-page report you'll never read, but a one-page brief with action items.

How Scout works

Sweep. Diff. Brief. Alert.

Daily

Automated sweeps

Scout crawls each competitor's website, pricing pages, product catalogs, job boards, Meta ad library, Google Ads transparency center, and major review platforms. Every page is snapshotted and stored for historical comparison.

Continuous

Change detection & classification

Scout doesn't just detect any change — it classifies changes by materiality. A footer copyright update is noise. A pricing page restructure is signal. New ad creative targeting your keywords is urgent. You only hear about what matters.

Monday

Weekly brief

Every Monday morning, a one-page brief lands in your inbox or Slack. What changed, why it matters, and what you should consider doing about it. Written for a founder, not an analyst. Three minutes to read, actionable immediately.

Instant

Real-time alerts

When something material happens between weekly briefs — a price drop, a new product launch, a sudden spike in negative reviews — Scout sends an immediate alert to Slack with context and a suggested response.
