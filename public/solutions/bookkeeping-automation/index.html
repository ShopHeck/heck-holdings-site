The problem

Bookkeeping shouldn't take this long.

Every month, your bookkeeper manually downloads bank statements, sorts through a pile of invoices and receipts, cross-references each transaction, enters categories, reconciles account by account, and hunts for the inevitable $47.23 discrepancy that doesn't match anything. For e-commerce businesses with hundreds of daily transactions, this is a full-time job disguised as a monthly task.

Reconcile does the grunt work. Every transaction parsed, matched, categorized, and reconciled — continuously, not monthly. Your bookkeeper spends their time on the exceptions, the strategy, and the judgment calls that actually need a human brain.

How it works

Ingest. Match. Categorize. Flag.

Ingest

Parse every document

Invoices, receipts, bank statements, credit card feeds — Reconcile ingests them all. OCR handles scanned PDFs and photos of receipts. Structured data (CSV, API feeds) is pulled directly. Every data point is extracted: vendor, date, amount, line items, tax, currency.

Match

Reconcile transactions

Each bank transaction is matched against invoices, POs, and receipts. Reconcile handles partial payments, split transactions, currency conversions, and timing differences. Matches are logged with confidence scores — high-confidence matches are auto-posted, low-confidence go to review.

Categorize

Chart of accounts mapping

Every transaction is categorized to your chart of accounts — learned from your historical data, not generic rules. New vendors are classified based on industry, transaction patterns, and context. Categories improve with every correction your bookkeeper makes.

Flag

Variance & exception reporting

Unmatched transactions, unusual amounts, duplicate invoices, missing receipts, and unexpected vendors are flagged for human review. Your bookkeeper gets a prioritized exception queue — not a full reconciliation to redo, just the items that actually need attention.
