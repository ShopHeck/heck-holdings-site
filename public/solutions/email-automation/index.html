The problem

Email is where your team's time goes to die.

The average knowledge worker spends 28% of their day on email. For service firms — law offices, agencies, consulting shops — it's worse. Every email needs to be read, classified, routed, and most of them need a reply. Your highest-paid people are doing data entry disguised as communication.

Mailroom doesn't just filter spam. It understands your email. It knows the difference between a new client inquiry and a scheduling change. It drafts replies that sound like you — not like a bot. And it opens matters, creates tasks, and routes escalations before your team even wakes up.

How Mailroom works

Read. Classify. Draft. Route. Repeat.

Step 1

Ingest & classify

Every inbound email is read in real time. Mailroom classifies intent (new inquiry, scheduling, billing, support, spam), identifies urgency (P1–P4), and extracts key entities — names, dates, amounts, case numbers. All of this happens in under 2 seconds.

Step 2

Draft & tone-match

For emails that need a response, Mailroom drafts a reply calibrated to your voice. Formal for client communication, casual for internal. Warm for support, direct for billing. It learns your tone from your sent mail — not from a generic prompt template.

Step 3

CRM & task creation

New client? Matter opened in Clio. Sales inquiry? Lead created in HubSpot. Support ticket? Card created in Linear. Mailroom pushes structured data into your existing systems — no manual data entry, no copy-paste between tabs.

Step 4

Route & escalate

Routine emails are auto-replied (with your approval workflow). Complex or sensitive emails are routed to the right person with a summary and suggested response. P1 escalations go to Slack immediately. Nothing falls through the cracks.
