The loop

Five steps, fully automated.

Your content engine runs on a continuous cycle. AI does steps 1–4. You do step 5. That's it.

01

Monitor

AI scans your niche 24/7 — competitor content, trending topics, Reddit threads, industry news, Google Trends — and identifies what your audience is talking about right now.

02

Plan

Topics are scored by relevance, search volume, and competition. AI builds a content calendar with post types, formats, and publishing dates — aligned to your brand strategy.

03

Create

AI drafts full posts, articles, and captions in your brand voice. LinkedIn thought leadership, blog posts with images, Instagram captions, Twitter threads — ready for review.

04

Queue

Drafts land in your inbox or Slack with one-tap approve/edit buttons. Everything organized by platform, date, and status. Your editorial dashboard.

05

Publish

Approve → published immediately. Or approve → scheduled for the optimal posting time. Edits? Make changes and re-approve. You always have final say.

Intelligence layer

AI doesn't guess what to write about.

Your content engine continuously monitors these signals to identify what your audience cares about — before your competitors notice.

🔍

Competitor content tracking

AI monitors competitor blogs, social accounts, and newsletters. When they publish something that gains traction, your engine surfaces it with a recommendation to create a better version or a counter-perspective.

📈

Google Trends & search data

Rising search queries in your niche get flagged automatically. AI identifies topic gaps — things people are searching for that nobody's writing about well — and adds them to your content calendar.

💬

Reddit & community signals

AI scans relevant subreddits, forums, and community boards for recurring questions and pain points. The best content comes from real problems real people are discussing — not keyword tools.

Industry news & events

New regulation? Product launch? Industry scandal? AI catches it immediately and drafts a timely take. Being first with commentary builds thought leadership faster than anything else.
