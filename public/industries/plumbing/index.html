The reality

In plumbing, response time is everything.

Plumbing is the most time-sensitive home service trade. A burst pipe causes $5,000+ in water damage per hour. A backed-up sewer makes a home uninhabitable. A failed water heater means no hot water for a family. These aren't "schedule for next week" problems — they're "I need someone right now" problems.

Studies show that 78% of customers hire the first service company that responds. Not the one with the best reviews. Not the one with the lowest price. The first one that picks up the phone and says "we can be there."

The average plumbing service call is worth $300–$600. Remodel and repipe jobs run $2,000–$15,000. Water heater replacements are $1,500–$4,000. Every call that goes to voicemail isn't just a lost lead — it's thousands of dollars walking to whoever picks up next.

Your plumbers can't answer the phone when they're under a sink, in a crawl space, or wrist-deep in a drain. But the homeowner with water spraying across their kitchen floor doesn't care about your staffing constraints. They care about who answers.

Emergency triage

Water emergencies need instant response — not voicemail.

Not every plumbing call is an emergency. A dripping faucet can wait. A burst pipe cannot. The problem with most answering solutions — human or automated — is that they treat every call the same. FrontDesk doesn't.

Every call gets triaged through plumbing-specific urgency criteria. The agent asks the questions a seasoned dispatcher would ask: Is there active water flowing? Have you shut off the main? Is it clean water or sewage? Is there electrical near the water? How long has it been going?

  • • Sewage backup into living space
  • • Gas line near water (safety risk)

Plumbing-specific scenarios

Built for how plumbing calls actually go.

Burst pipe at midnight

Homeowner wakes up to water in the basement. Panicked. Calls your number. FrontDesk answers, confirms the situation, and immediately walks them through finding and shutting off the main valve. While the homeowner handles the shutoff, the agent alerts you with all the details — address, situation, water shutoff status, accessibility. By the time you check your phone, the water is off and the customer is scheduled for first thing in the morning. They're not calling anyone else.

Water heater replacement pipeline

Customer calls about lukewarm water. FrontDesk captures the water heater type, age, fuel source, and symptoms. If the unit is 10+ years old, the intake data triggers ProposalForge to generate a replacement proposal alongside the service call estimate. Customer gets two options: repair and replace. The replacement proposal includes tank vs. tankless, energy savings, and available rebates. You're not just fixing a water heater — you're selling a $3,000–$6,000 job on every aging unit.

Remodel consultation capture

Homeowner calls about a bathroom remodel. This isn't an emergency — it's a high-value project ($5,000–$15,000+). FrontDesk captures the scope: which fixtures, how many bathrooms, budget range, timeline expectations, whether permits are needed. ProposalForge generates a preliminary estimate to set expectations. The on-site consultation is scheduled with all the details your estimator needs to walk in prepared, not blind.

Review generation after every job

Job complete. Tech marks it done. An automated follow-up goes to the customer: "How was your experience?" Happy customers get a direct link to leave a Google review — timed perfectly for when the positive experience is freshest. Unhappy customers get routed to you directly before they post a negative review publicly. Your Google rating climbs steadily and you catch problems before they become one-star reviews.
