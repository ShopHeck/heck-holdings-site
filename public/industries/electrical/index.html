The reality

Electrical work is too varied for a generic answering service.

Unlike HVAC or plumbing where most calls fit a few categories, electrical work spans an enormous range: outlet installations, panel upgrades, whole-house rewiring, EV charger installations, generator hookups, commercial tenant improvements, industrial controls, landscape lighting, and everything in between.

A generic receptionist — human or AI — can't properly qualify these calls. They don't know to ask about amperage when someone says "panel upgrade." They don't know that an EV charger install requires knowing the panel's available capacity. They don't ask whether a commercial job needs a licensed engineer's stamp.

The result: your estimator shows up to jobs blind, wastes time on-site figuring out what they should have known from the intake call, and quotes less accurately. Or worse — the wrong tech gets dispatched (a residential journeyman sent to a 3-phase commercial panel) and you eat a wasted truck roll.

AI agents built for electrical don't just answer the phone. They run the right intake for the right job type, route it to the right team, and generate an accurate initial estimate — all before the customer hangs up.

The routing problem

Residential and commercial need completely different workflows.

Residential workflow

  • 🏠 Homeowner describes the problem (often vaguely)
  • 🔌 FrontDesk asks targeted questions: breaker tripping? Which circuits? Panel age? Home age?
  • 📋 Job classified: service call, upgrade, new installation, or emergency
  • 💰 Flat-rate or T&M estimate generated automatically
  • 📅 Booked into residential schedule with the right tech
  • 📄 Permit requirements flagged if applicable

Commercial workflow

  • 🏢 Property manager or GC calls with project scope
  • ⚡ FrontDesk captures: voltage, phase, building type, timeline, budget range
  • 📋 Job classified: service/maintenance, tenant improvement, new construction, emergency
  • 💰 Preliminary estimate with engineer stamp costs if required
  • 📅 Routed to commercial team with scope summary
  • 📄 Insurance and licensing documentation prepared automatically

Electrical-specific scenarios

Built for the complexity of electrical work.

Panel upgrade pipeline

Homeowner calls about a breaker that keeps tripping. FrontDesk captures the panel brand, age, amperage, and which circuits are affected. If the panel is a Federal Pacific, Zinsco, or other recalled brand, it's flagged as an upgrade candidate immediately. ProposalForge generates a service call estimate plus a panel upgrade proposal with options. You're not just fixing a breaker trip — you're selling a $2,000–$4,000 upgrade on every qualifying panel.

EV charger lead capture

EV charger installations are the fastest-growing segment in residential electrical. FrontDesk captures the vehicle make (determines charging requirements), panel capacity, panel-to-garage distance, and whether a subpanel might be needed. ProposalForge generates a preliminary estimate covering the charger, circuit run, any panel upgrades needed, and permit fees. The customer gets a professional quote within minutes — not after a $150 on-site estimate fee.

Commercial tenant improvement intake

Property manager calls about a tenant buildout. FrontDesk runs the commercial intake: square footage, use type (office, retail, restaurant, warehouse), electrical requirements (lighting, data, specialty equipment), timeline, and budget range. The scope summary goes directly to your commercial estimator with everything needed to prepare a competitive bid — no wasted site visits to gather basic information.

Emergency power outage response

Customer calls — no power to part of the house. FrontDesk walks through diagnostics: Is the main breaker on? Any specific circuits out? Recent storm? Smell burning? Visible damage to the panel or wiring? Safety instructions if needed (don't touch the panel if there's water nearby). Emergency dispatch with full context so the electrician brings the right diagnostic equipment and likely replacement parts.
